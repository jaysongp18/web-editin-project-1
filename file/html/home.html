Brightness

Brightness is a term used to describe how overall light or dark an image is. To change the brightness of your image, use the Brightness slider. The difference in brightness between two objects or locations is known as contrast. To change the proportions of dark and light parts in your image, use the Contrast slide.

Saturation

In photography, the term ‘saturation’ describes the depth or intensity of colour present within an image. Saturation is also referred to as ‘chroma’; The more saturated an image is the more colourful and vibrant it will appear, less colour saturation will make an image appear subdued or muted.

Inversion

A photo technique known as color inversion converts all hues to their complementary hue. White, for instance, changes to black, green to magenta, and blue to orange.

Grayscale

The range of monochromatic (gray) hues, from pure white on the lightest end to pure black on the opposite end, is known as grayscale. The greatest luminance is white and the minimum luminance is black in a grayscale image since there is no color information present; everything in between is a shade of gray. Because of this, grayscale images have no color, just different shades of gray.

Rotate

One form of kinetic photography is camera rotation, which includes turning your camera completely around in a circle to produce an image with intriguing kaleidoscopic, geometric patterns.

Mirror

Mirror photography is another name for reflection photography. The fundamental idea behind it is to photograph a scene that also features a reflecting surface that reflects the surrounding landscape. It can occur in an environment that is as vast as the ocean or as minute as a raindrop.
